USPTO · Critical Warning
Trademark search: EDGE / EDGE TERMINAL
Likely conflicts. “EDGE” is registered across IC 09, 36, 42 by dozens of holders (Microsoft Edge browser, Edge Fitness, etc.). Run a clearance search via TESS before any signage spend. Recommend brand pivot or descriptor — “Edge Terminal” is more defensible than bare “Edge.”
Brand-risk note

The bare word EDGE is among the most-litigated marks in software (IC 009 / IC 042). A clean USPTO registration is unlikely without a distinctive modifier. Three viable paths in priority order:

1. Brand as “Edge Terminal” in all formal contexts — keep the $EDGE ticker as a sub-brand. This is the recommended posture.

2. Add an industry descriptor (“Edge Markets,” “Edge Signal,” “Edge Tape”) to clear distinctiveness without losing equity.

3. Skip federal registration; rely on common-law use + state-level filings. Acceptable for a token launch but fragile for a Series-A pitch.
